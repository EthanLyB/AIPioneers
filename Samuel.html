The Dawn of a New Era

Samuel's groundbreaking work happened at IBM, where he used the IBM 701, one of the first commercial computers. He saw its potential not just for calculations, but for thinking and learning.

The IBM 701 was huge and had limited memory, but Samuel's clever programs made it do amazing things.

The Checkers Master

In the 1950s, Samuel created a checkers program that could learn on its own. Instead of being told every move, the program improved by playing thousands of games against itself.

Rote Learning

The program memorized every board position and its outcome. It remembered what worked and what didn't.

Generalization

It learned to evaluate new board positions and apply its knowledge to situations it had never seen before.

A Lasting Legacy

Samuel's work was so revolutionary that he coined the term "machine learning" to describe it. He showed the world that computers could learn without being explicitly programmed for every single task.

"The field of study that gives computers the ability to learn without explicitly being programmed."

From Checkers to Today

Samuel's ideas laid the foundation for modern AI. His work with reinforcement learning is used in everything from self-driving cars to game-playing AI like AlphaGo. He showed us that a machine can become an expert through practice.
